What is Copyright? Creative Commons License Selector Extras + Citations

Creative Commons

Creative commons is a nonprofit dedicated to strengthening the "thriving commons of shared knowledge". CC offers licenses and a variety of other legal tools to help the public effectively share their work to others. Their licenses are easy to understand and completely free-to-use. (Creative Commons)

CC Licenses

Above are the 5 creative commons licenses, including the creative commons logo. They are (in order from left to right) BY, SA, NC, ND, and CC0 or public domain. BY enables others to remix, distribute, etc. with your work, as long as they give proper attribution. SA means that if you distribute the work, it must be under identical terms as the original. NC means that people who distribute your work can't monetize it. ND is the most restrictive, meaning that users can't edit your work at all. Lastly CC0 is an official public domain certification from creative commons. You can combine these terms to make specific licenses, like BY-SA, BY-NC-ND, etc. If you know you need a CC license but don't know exactly which one, try out the "License Selector" at the top of this website. (Creative Commons)

Other Forms of Copyright

Outside of creative commons there are other licenses, most notably the "All Rights Reserved" term. As you might believe, tihs copyright term is used to denote that the creator has control over all distribution and modification of their work. These kinds of copyright usually have you pay a fee to the government copyright office (in the United States, the U.S. Copyright Office) in order to properly register your ownership of a work.
